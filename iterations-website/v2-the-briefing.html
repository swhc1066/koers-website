V.

The method, for racers.

Mia is built from a small stack of well-tested ideas. Below: the five questions amateur racers ask in their first month, and what the literature says.
Q · 01 — Method

Why polarized? My current plan is sweet-spot.

Seiler's '10 work and successor papers found that elites distribute 80% of training time below LT1 and 20% above LT2 — a "polarized" model. For amateur racers with limited hours, sweet-spot works for a block, then plateaus. Koers builds the base polarized, then adds the race-specific intensity in the last six weeks. Translation: more easy and more hard, less middle.
Q · 02 — TSB

What's the right TSB on race day?

For an A-race: between +5 and +15 — fresh, but not stale. For a gravel epic like Unbound where survival matters more than top-end: closer to +20. Mia tapers to your race-specific TSB target, never a generic one. You'll see your projected TSB on race-day in the briefing.
Q · 03 — Volume

I have 8 hours a week. Can I still race Unbound?

Yes, for the finish. The 4.5-hour Cat 3 racer and the 14-hour Unbound finisher are the same person on different weeks. Koers prioritizes the long Saturday and one quality midweek session. If you have 10 hours: add a second quality day. The plan is built for your hours, not the pro's.
Q · 04 — FTP

How do you test FTP without ruining a week?

Koers uses a built-in 20-min effort at week 3 — not as a standalone "test day" but inside an opener workout that wasn't going to be easy anyway. Critical Power model on top, recalibrated every 28 days from your real ride data. You won't see a "test day" on the calendar; the test is in the data.
Q · 05 — Coaches

I already have a coach. Is Koers redundant?

No. Coach mode lets your coach write the plan and Koers handle the daily layer: the recovery read, the briefing, the day-of-ride note. Your coach sees everything Koers sees — your TSB, your edits, your conversation with Mia. ~340 coaches use Koers as their journal layer.
Q · 06 — Free month

What happens if I don't continue?

You keep the plan you have. You stop getting new ones. You can re-up any time. There's no streak to lose, no badges to expire, no notifications begging you to come back. If the plan stops being useful, stop.
